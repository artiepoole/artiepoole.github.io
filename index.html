Hackathons and Programming

In the past two years I have attended several Major League Hacking events, called hackathons, in which participants form teams and write the best program they can in the space of 24hrs. These are amazing oppotunities to learn how to code and to meet people from different universities, and sometimes even countries. The events are usually sponsored by major tech companies such as Dell or Intel and PayPal.

In the first hackathon I ever attended, my team and I designed an app to roll multiple dice on a Pebble Smartwatch. A string of letters and operators were typed on an app on the phone and then at the press of a button it would be sent to the watch. The watch would then roll the dice when the watch detected a sharp movement (such as the act of rolling dice) and would generate and display the result from the appropriate dice rolls. According to evidence, many of the judges from Pebble played roleplaying games and thought this idea was brilliant, because the team all won the category and we all recieved Pebble Classic smartwatches.
